Client & Developer Report

Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I decided what content to include by focusing on the purpose of my website: offering clear and simple videogame recommendations for both PC and mobile players. I chose images that matched the games I wanted to highlight and that visually represented each section of the site. My goal was to make the site easy to navigate and visually appealing without overwhelming the user.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I communicated with my developer mainly through written instructions and feedback. The communication was clear overall, and the developer understood the requirements of the project. There were no major issues, although sometimes I needed to clarify details about the layout or the content organization.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, the developer met my expectations. The site followed the plan, included the correct pages, and matched the wireframes. The final result was functional, organized, and visually consistent with what I had envisioned.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

Next time, I will provide more detailed examples and maybe sketches of what I want. I also plan to give feedback earlier in the process to avoid last‑minute changes. Clearer communication from the beginning can make the development process even smoother.

Developer Report

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

I communicated with the client by reviewing the site plan, asking clarifying questions, and following the instructions provided. The communication was smooth, and the client responded clearly. There were no major issues, although some parts of the plan required interpretation to match the final design.

How did you handle publishing your client's site and sharing it with the client?

I used GitHub Pages to publish the site. After pushing the files to the repository, I enabled GitHub Hosting and verified that each page loaded correctly. Once everything was working, I shared the live link with the client so they could review the final version.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

Managing the site with GitHub was a positive experience. GitHub Pages made publishing simple, and version control helped keep the project organized. It was easy to update files and immediately see changes reflected online.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part was organizing the content across multiple pages while keeping the layout consistent. I overcame this by following the wireframes closely and testing the site frequently to ensure everything looked correct.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed seeing the site come together visually. Adding images, styling the layout, and watching the design match the plan was very satisfying. Publishing the site and seeing it live online was also exciting.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would spend more time planning the visual style before coding. Creating a more detailed design guide could make the development process faster and more organized.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes, I feel more confident. This project helped me understand how to follow a site plan, communicate with a client, and publish a website professionally. It gave me practical experience with GitHub, HTML structure, and project organization.
